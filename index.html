Health Inequality in Victoria

A socio-economic analysis of the relationship between SEIFA index and adult obesity rates.

1. The Wealth-Health Gradient

The following scatter plot illustrates the correlation between socio-economic advantage (SEIFA) and obesity. A visible downward trend confirms that regions with higher economic advantage generally report lower rates of obesity.

2. High-Burden Regions

These Local Government Areas (LGAs) represent the regions with the highest recorded obesity rates. These areas may require targeted public health intervention and resource allocation.

3. Distribution of Economic Advantage

This histogram shows the distribution of SEIFA scores across Victoria. It highlights that the majority of regions cluster within a specific range of economic status.

4. Health Outcomes by Economic Decile

When grouping regions by their economic decile, a "health gradient" emerges. The data shows that health outcomes are structural, not just individual, following clear economic strata.

5. Population Density and Obesity Risk

This visualization maps the obesity rate against the number of residents. It helps identify if high-density populations face a higher obesity risk compared to smaller, rural communities.
